## History & Tradition

Arima Onsen boasts a history stretching back over a thousand years, with evidence suggesting its use even earlier. Legend has it that the hot springs were discovered by the Buddhist monk **Gyoki** during the Nara period (710-794 AD). The springs were later patronized by emperors and nobles, solidifying Arima's reputation as a premier onsen resort.

While the exact history of the Arima Grand Hotel itself is more recent, its foundations are firmly rooted in the traditions of Arima Onsen. The hotel has strived to preserve and enhance the onsen experience for its guests, drawing inspiration from the town's rich heritage. The design and ambiance of the hotel pay homage to the historical significance of Arima Onsen, creating a sense of continuity with the past. You will notice traditional Japanese art and decor incorporated into the hotel’s modern design.

The area gained prominence during the **Heian period** when emperors and aristocratic families favored the location. During the Edo Period, it continued to be a popular resort. The Meiji era ushered in modernization, but Arima Onsen managed to maintain its traditional appeal.

## Deep Dive into the Baths

The heart of Arima Grand Hotel lies in its exceptional onsen facilities. The hotel offers both indoor and outdoor baths, providing guests with a variety of bathing experiences. The highlight, of course, is the use of Arima Onsen's famous **Kinsen** and **Ginsen** waters.

### Kinsen (Gold Water)

The **Kinsen** is a ferruginous (iron-rich) spring known for its distinctive reddish-brown color, which it acquires upon exposure to air. This color gives it its name – "gold water." The high iron content is believed to be beneficial for a variety of ailments, including:

*   **Improving Circulation:** The iron helps to stimulate blood flow.
*   **Treating Skin Conditions:** The minerals are thought to have therapeutic properties for skin problems like eczema.
*   **Relieving Muscle Pain:** The warmth and mineral content can help to relax muscles and reduce pain.

The Kinsen at Arima Grand Hotel is sourced directly from the natural springs. The outdoor Kinsen bath offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains (depending on the bath and season), allowing you to soak in the therapeutic waters while immersing yourself in the beauty of nature. The water temperature is carefully maintained to ensure a comfortable and relaxing experience. You may find that after soaking in the **Kinsen** your skin feels smoother.

### Ginsen (Silver Water)

The **Ginsen** is a clear, colorless spring containing radium and carbonate. It is known for its more subtle, yet equally beneficial properties. Unlike the Kinsen, the Ginsen doesn't change color upon contact with air. Its benefits include:

*   **Boosting the Immune System:** Radium is believed to have a stimulating effect on the immune system.
*   **Improving Digestive Health:** The carbonate content may aid in digestion.
*   **Relaxing the Nervous System:** The gentle properties of the Ginsen are said to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

The Ginsen bath at Arima Grand Hotel offers a serene and calming atmosphere. Often, these baths are indoors because the benefits of Ginsen are derived from inhalation of the steam as much as submersion in the water itself. Many believe it has a calming and de-stressing effect.

### Bathing Etiquette

It is crucial to observe proper onsen etiquette to ensure a respectful and enjoyable experience for all guests. This includes:

*   **Showering Before Entering:** Always shower and thoroughly cleanse your body before entering the bath. Shampoo, conditioner, and body soap are provided.
*   **No Swimsuits:** Bathing is typically done in the nude.
*   **Towels:** Use the small towel provided for washing and modesty. Do not submerge the towel in the bathwater.
*   **No Loud Talking:** Maintain a quiet and respectful demeanor.
*   **Tattoos:** While some onsen still restrict guests with tattoos, Arima Grand Hotel is generally accepting, especially if tattoos can be covered with stickers or bandages. It is always best to inquire in advance.

### View from the Baths

The views from the onsen vary depending on the location and time of year. The outdoor baths offer the most spectacular scenery, with panoramic vistas of the surrounding mountains and valleys. In the spring, you can enjoy the beauty of cherry blossoms; in the autumn, the vibrant colors of the fall foliage. Even in the winter, the snow-covered landscape provides a unique and enchanting experience. The indoor baths, while not offering the same expansive views, provide a sense of privacy and tranquility.

### Kaiseki Dinner

The **Kaiseki dinner** is a multi-course Japanese meal that showcases the best of local ingredients and culinary artistry. Each dish is carefully prepared and presented, reflecting the season and the chef's creativity. A typical Kaiseki dinner at Arima Grand Hotel might include:

*   **Sakizuke:** A small appetizer to stimulate the appetite.
*   **Hassun:** A selection of seasonal delicacies, beautifully arranged on a platter.
*   **Mukozuke:** Freshly sliced sashimi, served with soy sauce and wasabi.
*   **Takiawase:** A simmered dish of vegetables, meat, or fish.
*   **Yakimono:** Grilled fish or meat.
*   **Nimono:** A boiled dish of vegetables, meat, or fish.
*   **Shokuji:** Rice, miso soup, and pickles.
*   **Mizumono:** A seasonal dessert.

The Kaiseki dinner is not just a meal; it is a culinary journey that engages all the senses. Each dish is a work of art, carefully crafted to delight the palate and showcase the beauty of Japanese cuisine. The ingredients are sourced locally whenever possible, ensuring the freshest and most flavorful experience.

## Access Guide

Arima Onsen is easily accessible by public transportation from Kobe and Osaka.

*   **From Kobe:** Take the Hokushin Kyuko Railway to Tanigami Station, then transfer to the Kobe Electric Railway to Arima Onsen Station. The journey takes about 30-40 minutes.
*   **From Osaka:** Take the JR Tokaido Line to Osaka Station, then transfer to the Hankyu Railway to Rokko Station. From Rokko Station, take the Hankyu Bus to Arima Onsen. The journey takes about 1 hour.

Alternatively, you can take a direct bus from Osaka or Kobe to Arima Onsen.

Once you arrive at Arima Onsen Station, the Arima Grand Hotel is a short walk or taxi ride away. The hotel also offers a free shuttle service from the station.
