## Introduction

In the verdant embrace of Oita Prefecture lies Yufuin, a town revered for its artistic sensibilities, tranquil atmosphere, and, above all, its nourishing hot springs. Within this idyllic setting, Yufuin Bettei Itsuki emerges not merely as a ryokan, but as a private sanctuary—a testament to refined luxury and profound serenity. This exclusive property distinguishes itself through its dedication to privacy, offering each guest a standalone villa complete with its own open-air onsen and commanding views of the majestic Mount Yufu. Itsuki crafts an experience where the natural beauty of Kyushu merges seamlessly with impeccable service and architectural elegance, appealing to the discerning traveler seeking a secluded escape from the quotidian.

The allure of Itsuki lies in its promise of an intimate connection with nature, without sacrificing an ounce of comfort. Here, the rhythm of the day is dictated by the sun tracking over the mountains, the gentle murmur of the breeze, and the soothing steam rising from your private bath. It is a place designed for quiet contemplation, for reconnecting with oneself or a cherished companion, all while immersed in the timeless rituals of Japanese hospitality.

## History & tradition

While Yufuin Bettei Itsuki represents a contemporary pinnacle of luxury ryokan experiences, its philosophy is deeply rooted in the enduring traditions of Japanese hospitality, or *omotenashi*. Unlike historical establishments with centuries of lineage, Itsuki embodies a more modern interpretation of ryokan culture, one that meticulously blends traditional aesthetics with an uncompromising commitment to privacy and bespoke comfort.

The vision behind Itsuki was to create a space that respects Yufuin's reputation as a haven of tranquility and artistic inspiration. Its design ethos reflects the profound Japanese appreciation for nature, integrating seamlessly into the surrounding landscape rather than dominating it. The architecture and landscaping draw inspiration from classic Japanese garden principles, where every vista is thoughtfully composed, and every material chosen for its innate beauty and harmonious presence.

The spirit of *omotenashi* is palpable in every interaction, from the warm, discreet welcome upon arrival to the attentive yet unobtrusive service throughout your stay. It is about anticipating needs before they are articulated, offering genuine warmth, and ensuring an experience that feels both deeply personal and effortlessly smooth. Guests are invited to partake in the cherished customs of ryokan stays, from donning exquisite yukata to savoring multi-course kaiseki meals, all presented with a contemporary finesse that aligns with Itsuki's sophisticated character. This blend of timeless tradition and modern luxury defines Itsuki's unique position in Yufuin's esteemed ryokan landscape.

## Baths & water

The onsen experience at Yufuin Bettei Itsuki is, without exaggeration, the crown jewel of any stay. Each of the meticulously designed villas boasts its own private, open-air bath (*rotenburo*), ensuring an unparalleled level of intimacy and relaxation. This commitment to privacy means guests can indulge in the therapeutic waters at their leisure, day or night, under the open sky, without concern for public bath etiquette or scheduling.

The water itself is a testament to Yufuin's natural bounty. Sourced directly from the town's renowned hot springs, it is typically a simple hot spring (weak alkaline), characterized by its crystal-clear appearance, gentle feel, and absence of strong sulfurous odors. This mild water is highly regarded for its skin-softening properties, leaving the body feeling supple and refreshed. Beyond the physical benefits, the onsen's warmth is profoundly relaxing, melting away tension and inviting a deep sense of calm.

Imagine immersing yourself in the steaming water, the cool mountain air caressing your skin, as you gaze upon the iconic silhouette of Mount Yufu. Many of the private baths are strategically positioned to offer these breathtaking views, creating a truly immersive sensory experience. The bath areas themselves are designed with natural materials—often local stone and fragrant cypress wood (*hinoki*)—blending harmoniously with the surrounding mini-gardens or natural vistas. Some feature expansive stone tubs, while others might incorporate wooden elements, all designed for comfort and aesthetic pleasure.

For guests with tattoos, the private onsen in each villa offers complete freedom. There are no public bath restrictions to consider, allowing everyone to enjoy the traditional Japanese onsen experience with absolute peace of mind and personal comfort. The combination of pristine water, stunning natural surroundings, and complete privacy makes Itsuki's onsen offerings a truly sublime and unforgettable highlight.

## Rooms & architecture

Yufuin Bettei Itsuki distinguishes itself through its architectural philosophy, which prioritizes space, privacy, and an intimate connection with nature. Rather than a single large building, the property comprises a collection of detached villas, each a self-contained haven designed to offer an exclusive retreat. This "bettei" style—meaning a separate villa or annex—underscores the ryokan's commitment to creating a highly personal and tranquil experience for every guest.

Each villa is a masterful blend of traditional Japanese aesthetic and modern comfort. The interiors typically feature spacious layouts, employing natural materials such as tatami matting, smooth wooden floors, and elegant shoji screens that filter natural light. The design ethos is one of understated luxury, where meticulous craftsmanship and thoughtful details speak louder than ostentatious displays. Furniture is often custom-made, providing both comfort and a sense of minimalist grace that allows the natural surroundings to take center stage.

The centerpiece of every villa, beyond the private onsen, is the panoramic view. Whether it's the majestic peak of Mount Yufu rising against the sky or the meticulously curated garden unique to each villa, the integration of indoor and outdoor spaces is seamless. Large windows and expansive terraces invite the natural landscape inward, blurring the lines between the private sanctuary and the verdant world outside.

Amenities within the rooms are carefully curated to enhance relaxation. Guests can expect comfortable futon bedding laid out on tatami floors or plush Western-style beds, high-quality yukata and sleepwear, and modern conveniences discreetly integrated into the traditional setting. Thoughtful touches like fragrant bath amenities, a selection of local teas, and carefully chosen artwork elevate the overall experience, ensuring that every moment spent within the villa is one of profound comfort and aesthetic pleasure. The architecture of Itsuki is not merely about shelter; it is about creating an immersive environment that soothes the spirit and delights the senses.

## Dining

The culinary journey at Yufuin Bettei Itsuki is an exquisite exploration of Kyushu's seasonal bounty, presented with the refined artistry of traditional Japanese kaiseki. Dinner is a multi-course spectacle, a testament to the chefs' dedication to both flavor and visual presentation. The philosophy is deeply rooted in *shun*, the celebration of peak seasonality, ensuring that every ingredient is at its freshest and most flavorful.

Typically, the dining experience at Itsuki is designed for ultimate privacy and comfort, with meals often served in a private dining room or, for an even more intimate setting, within the guest's own villa. This allows for an uninterrupted appreciation of each dish, presented with grace and accompanied by the serene ambiance.

The kaiseki menu showcases a harmonious balance of textures, temperatures, and tastes. Highlights often include locally sourced ingredients, such as the renowned Bungo beef, celebrated for its tender marbling and rich umami flavor, prepared in various traditional and innovative ways. Fresh seafood from nearby Beppu Bay is another staple, presented as delicate sashimi or lightly grilled to perfection. Seasonal vegetables, often organically grown in the fertile lands around Yufuin, add vibrant colors and earthy notes to the intricate preparations.

Each course, from the delicate *sakizuke* (appetizer) to the hearty *nimono* (simmered dish), the crisp *agemono* (fried dish), and finally the dessert, is a small masterpiece. The presentation is as important as the taste, with dishes arranged on exquisite ceramics and lacquerware, evoking the beauty of the season. A carefully curated selection of local sake and shochu is available to complement the meal, enhancing the regional flavors.

Breakfast, typically a traditional Japanese affair, is equally meticulous. Expect a nourishing spread of grilled fish, delicate tamagoyaki (rolled omelet), miso soup, steamed rice, and a variety of pickled vegetables, all designed to gently awaken the palate and prepare you for the day ahead in Yufuin. The dining experience at Itsuki is not merely sustenance; it is a profound cultural immersion and an unforgettable feast for the senses.

## Local area

Yufuin, nestled in the heart of Oita Prefecture, offers a distinctive charm that sets it apart from more bustling onsen towns. It is a place that celebrates nature, art, and a relaxed pace of life, making it the perfect complement to a stay at Yufuin Bettei Itsuki.

The town itself is a delightful place for leisurely strolls. The main street, leading from Yufuin Station towards Lake Kinrinko, is lined with quaint boutiques, artisan workshops, and inviting cafes. Here, one can discover unique local crafts, handmade ceramics, and gourmet treats, reflecting Yufuin's reputation as a creative hub. Unlike some tourist areas, Yufuin maintains a refined, unhurried atmosphere, encouraging visitors to savor the moment.

Lake Kinrinko is a particularly picturesque spot, especially enchanting in the early morning mist. The lake is famous for its combination of hot and cold spring waters, which cause steam to rise from its surface during cooler seasons, creating a mystical spectacle. Surrounding the lake are charming walking paths, small shrines, and cafes where one can relax and enjoy the serene views.

For those inclined towards more active pursuits, the majestic Mount Yufu, which forms a dramatic backdrop to the town, offers hiking opportunities. Various trails cater to different fitness levels, rewarding climbers with panoramic views of the Yufuin basin and beyond.

Beyond Yufuin itself, the region offers further exploration. The hot spring city of Beppu, famous for its "Hells of Beppu" (jigoku meguri) with their vibrant colors and steaming geological phenomena, is a relatively short drive away, offering a contrast to Yufuin's tranquil ambiance. Oita Prefecture is also renowned for its local cuisine, including freshly caught seafood and the aforementioned Bungo beef, which can be enjoyed in various local establishments. A stay at Itsuki provides a luxurious base from which to immerse oneself in the gentle sophistication and natural beauty of this cherished corner of Kyushu.

## Practical tips

To ensure a seamless and enriching experience at Yufuin Bettei Itsuki, a few practical considerations are helpful for the discerning traveler.

**Reservations:** As an exclusive luxury ryokan with a limited number of villas, securing a reservation well in advance is highly recommended, particularly if planning a visit during peak seasons such as cherry blossom season (spring), autumn foliage, or national holidays. Booking directly through the ryokan's official website or a reputable luxury travel agent is often the most reliable method.

**Check-in/Check-out:** Typical check-in times are in the afternoon, often around 3:00 PM, allowing staff ample time to prepare your private villa. Check-out is usually around 11:00 AM. It is always advisable to confirm specific times when booking.

**Language:** While high-end ryokans like Itsuki often have staff with some English proficiency, particularly at the front desk, English communication may not be universally fluent among all team members. A polite demeanor, patience, and perhaps a translation app can be helpful. Learning a few basic Japanese phrases, such as "Arigato gozaimasu" (Thank you very much) or "Sumimasen" (Excuse me/Sorry), is always appreciated.

**Dress Code:** The atmosphere at Itsuki is one of relaxed elegance. While dining, you may wish to dress in smart casual attire. Within your villa and when moving around the property, the provided yukata (traditional Japanese garment) is perfectly appropriate and encouraged for an authentic ryokan experience. Comfortable footwear, easy to slip on and off, is also practical.

**Tattoo Policy:** With private onsen available in every villa, guests with tattoos can fully enjoy the hot spring experience without any concerns that might arise in public bath settings. This offers complete freedom and discretion.

**Seasonality:** Yufuin offers distinct beauty in every season. Spring brings cherry blossoms, summer is lush and green, autumn paints the mountains in vibrant hues, and winter often blankets the landscape in snow, creating a magical setting for onsen bathing. Consider which seasonal charm aligns best with your preferences.

**What to Bring:** Beyond personal essentials, pack light. The ryokan provides most amenities, including quality toiletries, hairdryers, and bathrobes/yukata. A good book or sketchbook might enhance the contemplative atmosphere.

## Access

Yufuin Bettei Itsuki is situated in the picturesque town of Yufuin, Oita Prefecture, on Japan's Kyushu island. Reaching this serene retreat is part of the experience, offering scenic journeys that prepare you for the tranquility ahead.

**Nearest Airports:**
*   **Fukuoka Airport (FUK):** This is the most common gateway for international travelers heading to Yufuin. It's well-connected and offers several convenient onward options.
*   **Oita Airport (OIT):** A closer regional airport, Oita Airport is ideal for domestic connections or for those who prefer a shorter final leg of the journey.

**From Fukuoka Airport (FUK):**
*   **By Train (Recommended for scenic beauty):** The most popular and enchanting way to reach Yufuin from Fukuoka is via the **JR Yufu no Mori** limited express train. This specially designed tourist train offers comfortable seating and large windows to appreciate the verdant Kyushu landscape. The journey typically takes approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes directly to Yufuin Station. Reservations are highly recommended as it's a very popular service.
*   **By Highway Bus:** Several highway bus companies operate direct services from Fukuoka Airport (and Hakata Station) to Yufuin Bus Center. This is often a slightly quicker and more economical option, with journey times typically around 1 hour and 45 minutes to 2 hours, depending on traffic.
*   **By Car:** Renting a car at Fukuoka Airport provides flexibility, allowing you to explore the wider Oita region. The drive to Yufuin takes roughly 1 hour and 30 minutes on expressways, though navigation can be challenging for those unfamiliar with Japanese roads.

**From Oita Airport (OIT):**
*   **By Airport Bus:** Direct airport bus services connect Oita Airport to Yufuin Bus Center. The journey is relatively short, typically around 55 minutes to 1 hour.

**Local Access in Yufuin:**
*   **From Yufuin Station/Bus Center:** Yufuin Bettei Itsuki is located a short distance from Yufuin Station and the Bus Center. While some guests may opt for a leisurely walk through the charming town, a taxi is the most convenient option, especially with luggage. Many luxury ryokans in Yufuin also offer a complimentary shuttle service from the station; it is advisable to check with Itsuki directly when making your reservation regarding their shuttle arrangements.

Regardless of your chosen mode of transport, the journey to Yufuin is an integral part of the experience, offering glimpses of Kyushu's natural splendor before you arrive at the exclusive sanctuary of Yufuin Bettei Itsuki.
