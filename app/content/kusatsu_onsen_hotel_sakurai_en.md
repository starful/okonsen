## Introduction

Kusatsu Onsen, a name that resonates with profound reverence among connoisseurs of Japan's hot spring culture, stands as an undisputed titan, consistently ranked among the nation's top three onsen destinations. At the very heart of this legendary spa town, overlooking its vibrant tapestry of steam and tradition, lies Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai. This distinguished ryokan is not merely an accommodation; it is an institution, a grand gateway to experiencing the potent, therapeutic waters of Kusatsu in an environment of sophisticated comfort and genuine hospitality.

From its commanding position, Hotel Sakurai offers guests breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding Gunma mountains, a verdant embrace that shifts with the seasons, from the soft greens of spring and vibrant hues of summer to the fiery golds of autumn and the pristine whites of winter. It is a place where the famed highly acidic spring water, celebrated for its purported healing properties for centuries, becomes the focal point of a restorative escape. Here, the meticulously refined traditions of a Japanese ryokan seamlessly intertwine with the unique, unpretentious charm of Kusatsu, creating an experience that is both deeply authentic and exquisitely indulgent. For the discerning traveler seeking to immerse themselves in the very essence of Japanese onsen culture, Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai presents itself as an unparalleled choice, a sanctuary where ancient legends are lived anew.

## History & tradition

Kusatsu Onsen's history is steeped in legend and documented fact, tracing its origins back well over a millennium. Revered by samurai and shoguns alike, its powerful waters were believed to possess miraculous healing capabilities, drawing visitors from across the archipelago. The town itself has evolved organically around its primary hot spring sources, particularly the iconic Yubatake, a captivating open-air hot water field that remains the pulsating heart of Kusatsu. This deep historical lineage has imbued Kusatsu with a tangible sense of tradition, evident in its unique *yumomi* hot water stirring ceremony and the timeless architecture of its bathhouses and ryokans.

Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai, while a modern establishment in its comprehensive amenities, is deeply rooted in this venerable tradition. It stands as a testament to the enduring appeal of Kusatsu's waters and the hospitality that has defined Japanese inn-keeping for generations. The ryokan’s design thoughtfully incorporates elements of traditional Japanese aesthetics, from its welcoming entrance to the careful placement of its public spaces, fostering an atmosphere of calm and respectful contemplation. Rather than presenting a starkly contemporary façade, Hotel Sakurai embraces the warmth and communal spirit that characterizes Kusatsu, creating a space where guests can connect with the town's storied past while enjoying the comforts of the present. The attentive service, the curated seasonal menus, and the meticulous maintenance of its bathing facilities all reflect a profound respect for the ryokan tradition and the legacy of Kusatsu as a healing destination.

## Baths & water

The essence of any Kusatsu experience lies in its extraordinary onsen waters, and Hotel Sakurai provides an exceptional conduit to this natural marvel. Kusatsu's springs are renowned globally for their remarkably high acidity, a characteristic attributed to the sulfur and various minerals dissolved within them. These waters typically register a pH of around 2.1, making them highly antibacterial and often described as having profound benefits for skin ailments, muscle pain, and general rejuvenation. The sensation of immersing oneself in Kusatsu's water is uniquely invigorating – a distinct warmth that penetrates deeply, often accompanied by a subtle tingle.

Hotel Sakurai offers a diverse array of bathing experiences, ensuring every guest can find their preferred sanctuary. The ryokan boasts multiple expansive public baths, both indoors and outdoors, each designed to maximize relaxation and connection with the natural surroundings. The large indoor baths, often crafted from stone and rich timber, provide a tranquil escape regardless of the weather, with ample space for quiet contemplation. However, the true gems are the *rotenburo*, the open-air baths. Here, guests can soak in the therapeutic waters while enveloped by the fresh mountain air, perhaps under a canopy of stars or amidst the gentle fall of snow. Some of these outdoor baths are strategically positioned to offer those "stunning views" mentioned in the title, looking out over the town or the distant peaks, transforming a simple bath into an unforgettable communion with nature.

The hot spring water at Hotel Sakurai is directly sourced from one of Kusatsu's prominent springs, often a blend to achieve optimal temperature and mineral balance, ensuring authenticity and potency. Beyond the public baths, the ryokan typically offers several private onsen rooms or *kashikiri-buro*. These secluded spaces are ideal for couples or families seeking an intimate bathing experience, allowing them to enjoy the healing waters in complete privacy, without reservation. Each private bath is distinct, perhaps featuring different materials like cypress wood or rock, and designed to offer a unique ambiance.

Regarding tattoo policy, while Kusatsu town itself is generally more accommodating than many other onsen regions in Japan, individual ryokans retain their own discretion. Hotel Sakurai, like many larger establishments, typically requests guests with visible tattoos to use the private onsen facilities when available, or to cover their tattoos while in public bathing areas, out of respect for traditional Japanese sensibilities. It is always prudent to confirm the specific policy when making a reservation to ensure a seamless and comfortable stay. The sheer variety and quality of the baths, coupled with the legendary properties of Kusatsu's water, make the onsen experience at Hotel Sakurai a true highlight, a cornerstone of its restorative allure.

## Rooms & architecture

Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai offers a spectrum of accommodations, primarily rooted in the classic Japanese ryokan aesthetic, yet often incorporating contemporary comforts. The majority of its rooms are traditional Japanese-style (和室, *washitsu*), characterized by their serene minimalism and authentic design. Guests stepping into these rooms are greeted by the comforting aroma of *tatami* mat flooring, a natural rush grass material that lends a distinctive soft texture underfoot. The sleeping arrangements typically involve *futon* bedding, laid out by staff in the evening and discreetly folded away in the morning, transforming the space for daytime use.

Central to each *washitsu* is the *tokonoma* alcove, an aesthetic focal point often adorned with a seasonal hanging scroll (*kakejiku*) and a delicate floral arrangement (*ikebana*), providing a glimpse into Japan's reverence for nature and art. *Shoji* paper screens and *fusuma* sliding doors, often featuring subtle patterns, separate the living areas and connect guests with the outdoors, allowing diffused light to filter in. Many rooms feature a *engawa* (veranda) or a seating area by the window, often with comfortable chairs and a low table, from which guests can enjoy the "stunning views" – whether it be the picturesque Kusatsu townscape, the verdant valley, or the majestic mountains beyond. These vantage points are particularly cherished, offering a dynamic canvas that changes with the time of day and the seasons.

Beyond the traditional Japanese rooms, Hotel Sakurai also typically offers Western-style rooms or hybrid Japanese-Western rooms, which combine the comforts of a bed with the charm of a tatami living area. These options cater to guests who might prefer a conventional bed while still wishing to experience a touch of Japanese design. Regardless of the specific room type, each accommodation is meticulously maintained and equipped with modern conveniences such such as air conditioning, televisions, and private en-suite bathrooms. Complimentary amenities usually include Yukata robes, suitable for lounging and wearing to the baths, and sets of green tea for a peaceful moment of relaxation. The overall architecture of the hotel, while extensive to accommodate its numerous facilities, maintains a harmonious balance, utilizing natural materials and thoughtful layouts to evoke a sense of tranquility and connection to its natural setting. The large windows throughout the property ensure that the beauty of Kusatsu's surroundings is always an integral part of the experience.

## Dining

The dining experience at Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai is a profound immersion into the culinary artistry of Gunma Prefecture, delivered through the exquisite medium of *kaiseki*. This traditional multi-course Japanese dinner is a testament to seasonality, local produce, and meticulous presentation, transforming a meal into a performance for all senses.

Typically served either in the ryokan's elegant dining hall or, for a more intimate experience, in a private dining room (which may be a separate space or even in the guest's own room, depending on the room category and plan), each *kaiseki* meal is a carefully orchestrated symphony of flavors, textures, and colors. The menu evolves with the changing seasons, ensuring that every ingredient is at its peak freshness and flavor. Expect a procession of beautifully plated dishes: a delicate appetizer platter (*sakizuke*), a clear soup (*suimono*), sashimi of pristine local fish, expertly grilled delicacies (*yakimono*), simmered dishes (*nimono*), often a seasonal tempura, and a staple of rice, miso soup, and pickles to conclude.

The chefs at Hotel Sakurai meticulously highlight ingredients indigenous to Gunma and its mountainous environs. This might include Jōshū wagyu beef, renowned for its tender marbling; freshwater fish from local rivers; a vibrant array of seasonal mountain vegetables (*sansai*); and locally grown rice. Each dish is not only a culinary delight but a visual masterpiece, arranged with precision and artistic flair, reflecting the Japanese appreciation for beauty in simplicity. The tableware itself is often a work of art, carefully chosen to complement the food.

Breakfast, typically a hearty Japanese affair (*washoku*), continues the theme of local bounty and nourishing goodness. Guests can anticipate grilled fish, tamagoyaki (rolled omelet), miso soup, steamed rice, various pickled vegetables, and an assortment of small side dishes, providing a wholesome and energizing start to the day. For those who prefer Western options, some ryokans of this scale may offer a small selection of Western-style items alongside the Japanese breakfast buffet. The dining experience at Hotel Sakurai is more than sustenance; it is a celebration of Japan's culinary heritage, a mindful journey through the flavors of Gunma, and an integral part of the luxury ryokan stay.

## Local area

Kusatsu Onsen is not merely a collection of baths; it is a vibrant, captivating town built around its famed hot springs, offering a rich tapestry of sights and experiences. Hotel Sakurai's location places guests within easy reach of these attractions, allowing for delightful exploration on foot.

The undisputed heart of Kusatsu is the **Yubatake (湯畑)**, or "hot water field." This iconic landmark is a truly unique spectacle: a vast open-air system of wooden flumes through which scalding hot spring water from the natural source cascades, cools, and is channeled to various ryokans and public baths. The air around Yubatake is perpetually thick with steamy mist and the distinctive sulfuric aroma of the springs, creating an otherworldly atmosphere, especially enchanting at night when it is beautifully illuminated. Visitors can observe the traditional *yumomi* ceremony nearby at the Netsunoyu building, where practitioners stir the hot water with long wooden paddles to cool it without diluting its potency, often accompanied by traditional folk songs.

A short, pleasant stroll from the Yubatake leads to **Sainokawara Park (西の河原公園)**, a natural wonderland where hot spring water bubbles directly from the earth, forming a river that flows through a landscape dotted with steaming pools. This expansive park offers numerous footbaths (*ashiyu*) where visitors can soak their feet in the therapeutic waters amidst nature's embrace. The park transforms dramatically with the seasons, from lush greenery to snow-covered tranquility, always offering a serene retreat.

Wandering through the charming streets radiating from Yubatake, visitors will discover a delightful array of traditional shops selling local crafts, souvenirs, and Kusatsu-specific delicacies. Sample *onsen manju* (steamed buns filled with sweet bean paste, often made with hot spring water), try *senbei* crackers, or browse for unique gifts. The aroma of local street food and the lively chatter of visitors create an inviting ambiance. Other notable spots include the **Kusatsu Onsen Furusato-kan**, a local museum showcasing the town's history, and various smaller public bathhouses (*sotoyu*) that offer a more rustic, local bathing experience. For a touch of nature, hiking trails in the surrounding mountains, like those around Mt. Shirane (though access varies by season due to volcanic activity), provide opportunities for more vigorous exploration and stunning vistas. The area around Kusatsu offers a quintessential Japanese onsen town experience, blending natural beauty with centuries of cultural heritage.

## Practical tips

To fully appreciate the refined experience at Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai and the charm of Kusatsu, a few practical considerations are beneficial for the discerning traveler.

Firstly, booking well in advance is highly recommended, especially for peak seasons such as autumn (for vivid foliage), winter (for snow-covered landscapes and skiing access), and Japanese national holidays. Kusatsu is an incredibly popular destination, and prime rooms, especially those with exceptional views or private baths, are often reserved months ahead. When booking, carefully review the various accommodation plans, as they often include specific dining options (e.g., standard *kaiseki* vs. premium menus) and room types.

Upon arrival, guests are typically welcomed with a warm greeting and often a cup of freshly brewed green tea and a small sweet. Check-in procedures are usually smooth and efficient. While traditional Japanese ryokans often have specific times for dinner service, Hotel Sakurai, being a larger establishment, may offer a range of dinner slots; it's advisable to confirm your preferred time upon check-in. It's customary to wear the provided *yukata* robe and *geta* sandals within the ryokan and when strolling around the town's central area, embracing the local custom.

For bathing, remember to thoroughly wash your body with soap and water before entering the hot spring baths, using the showering facilities provided in the bathing area. This is a fundamental aspect of Japanese onsen etiquette, ensuring cleanliness for all bathers. While the water is highly acidic, it is generally safe for consumption in small amounts if you are curious, though primary focus should be on external benefits. Kusatsu's atmosphere is generally relaxed and welcoming to foreign visitors, but a respectful attitude towards local customs will always enhance your experience. English-speaking staff are often available, particularly at larger ryokans like Hotel Sakurai, but having a translation app or a few basic Japanese phrases can be helpful for specific requests.

Payment is typically settled upon check-out. While credit cards are widely accepted at the hotel, carrying some Japanese Yen for smaller purchases in town, such as souvenirs or street food, is recommended. Embrace the pace of a ryokan stay – it’s an invitation to slow down, relax, and savor the tranquility and healing power of Japan's most celebrated hot springs.

## Access

Reaching Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai, nestled in the heart of Gunma Prefecture, requires a journey that is both scenic and straightforward, primarily leveraging Japan's efficient rail and bus network.

The most common approach for international travelers or those departing from Tokyo begins at **Tokyo Station** or **Shinjuku Station**. From these major hubs, take the **JR Limited Express Kusatsu** train directly to **Naganohara-Kusatsuguchi Station (長野原草津口駅)**. This train ride offers picturesque views of the Japanese countryside and typically takes approximately 2.5 to 3 hours. Alternatively, if departing from Ueno, the **JR Limited Express Akagi** can be used, with a transfer at Takasaki to a local line leading to Naganohara-Kusatsuguchi.

Once you arrive at Naganohara-Kusatsuguchi Station, the final leg of the journey to Kusatsu Onsen town, where Hotel Sakurai is located, is completed by **local bus**. Buses departing for Kusatsu Onsen align well with train arrivals and take about 25-30 minutes. These buses will drop passengers off at the **Kusatsu Onsen Bus Terminal**, which is centrally located within the town.

From the Kusatsu Onsen Bus Terminal, Hotel Sakurai is typically a short, pleasant walk, perhaps 5 to 10 minutes, depending on your pace and luggage. However, for the convenience of its guests, Hotel Sakurai usually provides a **complimentary shuttle service** from the Kusatsu Onsen Bus Terminal directly to the ryokan. It is advisable to check the shuttle schedule in advance or inquire upon arrival at the bus terminal to arrange for pick-up. This service ensures a smooth and effortless transition from the public transport network to the welcoming doors of the hotel, allowing you to quickly settle in and begin your restorative retreat. The journey itself becomes part of the experience, transitioning from urban bustle to the serene, refreshing air of the mountains.
