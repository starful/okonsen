## Introduction

Beppu, a name synonymous with Japan's most prolific hot springs, holds a unique place in the country's travel landscape. Within this geothermal wonderland, AMANE Resort Seikai stands as an exemplar of refined luxury, offering an experience that transcends the conventional ryokan stay. Perched majestically on the shoreline, this resort masterfully marries Beppu's ancient onsen tradition with cutting-edge design and an unwavering commitment to guest privacy. It is a sanctuary where the Pacific's boundless horizon meets the earth's nurturing warmth, a destination crafted for discerning travelers seeking both revitalization and sophisticated seclusion.

AMANE Seikai distinguishes itself with an all-suite, all-ocean-view configuration, each generously appointed with its own private outdoor onsen bath. This singular focus on personal indulgence, set against the breathtaking backdrop of Beppu Bay, elevates the concept of a hot spring retreat to an art form. Here, the journey is not merely about soaking in mineral-rich waters, but about an immersive sensory experience—the scent of the ocean breeze, the panoramic vista, the exquisite culinary creations, and the profound tranquility that permeates every space. It is, without question, one of Japan's most compelling destinations for luxury onsen connoisseurs.

## History & tradition

While AMANE Resort Seikai itself presents a thoroughly modern architectural and hospitality vision, its essence is deeply rooted in Beppu's illustrious history as an onsen town. Beppu boasts a heritage stretching back over a millennium, its geothermal activity documented in ancient texts, drawing visitors for centuries to its therapeutic waters. The town has evolved from a simple hot spring village to a bustling city, yet its core identity remains inextricably linked to the bountiful springs bubbling beneath its surface.

AMANE Seikai embodies a contemporary interpretation of this enduring tradition. Instead of replicating a classical wooden ryokan, it pays homage to Japanese aesthetics through sleek lines, natural materials, and an emphasis on light and space. The resort's design philosophy embraces *omotenashi*—the art of selfless hospitality—by anticipating every guest's need for comfort and privacy, a cornerstone of traditional Japanese innkeeping. It takes the fundamental pleasure of soaking in an onsen and elevates it to an unparalleled private experience, integrating the ocean's vastness into the intimate ritual of bathing. This fusion of ancient healing and modern luxury positions AMANE Seikai not merely as a hotel, but as a continuation and evolution of Beppu's storied onsen legacy. The deep respect for the source of the waters and the meticulous care in preparing each guest's stay echo the timeless principles of Japanese hospitality, reimagined for the contemporary world.

## Baths & water

The heart of the AMANE Resort Seikai experience lies undeniably in its exceptional onsen offerings, where the mineral-rich waters of Beppu are enjoyed in utmost privacy and luxury. The resort's most striking feature is that *every* guest suite is equipped with its own private, open-air onsen bath, overlooking the expansive panorama of Beppu Bay. This design choice represents the ultimate commitment to personal indulgence, allowing guests to immerse themselves in the healing waters at their leisure, bathed in natural light by day or under a canopy of stars at night, with the soothing sound of waves as their only companion.

The water itself is sourced directly from Beppu's famed springs, celebrated for their therapeutic properties. While the specific mineral composition can vary slightly across Beppu's many sources, the waters at AMANE Seikai are typically a sodium bicarbonate type, known for their silky texture and benefits for skin conditions, muscle aches, and fatigue. The heat is naturally consistent, providing a deeply penetrating warmth that promotes relaxation and rejuvenation. As you recline in your private bath, the minerals work their subtle magic, leaving your skin feeling smooth and your body utterly relaxed.

Beyond the private in-room baths, AMANE Seikai also features magnificent communal bathing facilities, often separated by gender. These public baths are designed with an equally stunning aesthetic, typically featuring large, expansive windows that maintain the connection to the ocean vista. Imagine an infinity-edge outdoor bath where the water's surface seems to merge seamlessly with the sea, creating an illusion of floating directly on the bay. The indoor baths are equally serene, offering a tranquil escape with the same high-quality spring water. These communal areas provide an opportunity to experience the traditional Japanese public bathing culture, albeit in a highly refined and spacious environment, allowing for quiet contemplation amidst breathtaking scenery. The meticulous hygiene and thoughtful amenities provided in all bathing areas underscore the resort's dedication to an impeccable onsen experience.

## Dining

Dining at AMANE Resort Seikai is an experience that rivals the breathtaking views and soothing onsen. The resort prides itself on its culinary artistry, showcasing the bounty of Oita Prefecture and the Seto Inland Sea through meticulously crafted kaiseki meals. Kaiseki, the multi-course traditional Japanese dinner, is presented here as an exquisite performance, where each dish is a miniature work of art, appealing to both the palate and the eye.

The culinary philosophy centers around *shun*, the Japanese concept of seasonality, ensuring that ingredients are at their peak of freshness and flavor. Local produce is championed, with a particular emphasis on seafood caught daily from Beppu Bay and the surrounding waters. Expect delicate sashimi, expertly grilled fish, and succulent shellfish, all prepared with precision and respect for their inherent qualities. Oita is also renowned for its Bungo beef, a highly prized wagyu known for its tender texture and rich marbling. This local delicacy often features prominently in the kaiseki menu, prepared in various ways to highlight its exceptional quality, perhaps as a seared steak or shabu-shabu.

Beyond the main ingredients, the chefs at AMANE Seikai demonstrate remarkable creativity in preparing accompanying dishes, from seasonal vegetables simmered in delicate broths to savory custards and crisp tempura. The presentation is as important as the taste, with each course arranged with an artistic flourish on beautiful ceramics, reflecting the colors and textures of the season.

Dinner is typically served in one of the resort's elegant dining spaces, often offering panoramic ocean views, or in private dining rooms, providing an intimate setting for guests to savor their meal. The ambiance is one of quiet sophistication, designed to enhance the appreciation of the culinary journey.

Breakfast is equally thoughtfully prepared, typically a traditional Japanese set meal (*washoku*) featuring an array of small dishes: grilled fish, steamed rice, miso soup, pickles, and various local specialties. It’s a nourishing and visually appealing start to the day, ensuring guests depart for their explorations feeling energized and satisfied. The resort's dedication to culinary excellence ensures that dining is an integral and unforgettable part of the AMANE Seikai experience, reflecting the richness of Oita's gastronomic landscape.

## Local area

Beppu, the vibrant city that hosts AMANE Resort Seikai, is far more than just a collection of hot springs; it is a dynamic hub of geothermal activity, culture, and natural beauty. Venturing beyond the resort's luxurious confines reveals a destination rich in unique experiences.

The most iconic attraction in Beppu is undoubtedly the "Hells of Beppu" (*Jigoku Meguri*). These seven spectacular hot springs are too hot for bathing but offer a mesmerizing spectacle of nature's raw power. Each hell boasts a distinct character, from the cobalt-blue "Umi Jigoku" (Sea Hell) and the blood-red "Chinoike Jigoku" (Blood Pond Hell) to the geyser-like "Tatsumaki Jigoku" (Spout Hell) and the mud-bubbling "Oniishibozu Jigoku" (Demon's Head Hell). Exploring these geological wonders is a fascinating journey, showcasing the incredible diversity of Beppu's geothermal landscape.

For those interested in historical onsen culture, a visit to Takegawara Onsen is highly recommended. This beautiful wooden bathhouse, dating back to 1879, offers a glimpse into traditional bathing, including unique sand baths where you are buried in naturally heated sand. It’s a wonderfully nostalgic experience, contrasting with the modernity of AMANE Seikai.

Beyond the springs, Beppu offers panoramic views. The Beppu Tower, an observation tower that predates Tokyo Tower, provides a 360-degree vista of the city, mountains, and bay. For a more natural elevation, the Beppu Ropeway ascends Mount Tsurumi, offering stunning seasonal scenery, from vibrant autumn leaves to crisp winter snow, and breathtaking views of the city below and as far as Shikoku on clear days.

The city also boasts a bustling local market culture, where you can find fresh seafood, local produce, and traditional crafts. Wandering through the backstreets can reveal charming local eateries and artisan shops. Beppu is a city that thrives on the juxtaposition of ancient natural wonders and a lively urban atmosphere, offering a compelling blend of relaxation, exploration, and cultural immersion for every traveler.

## Practical tips

To ensure a seamless and enriching stay at AMANE Resort Seikai, a few practical considerations are worth noting for the discerning traveler.

**Reservations:** Given the resort's exclusive nature and popularity, particularly for its unique private onsen suites, securing reservations well in advance is highly advisable. Peak seasons, Japanese holidays, and weekends tend to book out quickly. Online booking platforms or direct contact with the resort are the typical methods.

**Check-in and Check-out:** Standard check-in times are usually in the afternoon, often around 3:00 PM, while check-out is typically around 11:00 AM. It's always best to confirm these details when booking, and the resort may offer luggage storage for early arrivals or late departures.

**Dress Code:** While AMANE Resort Seikai embodies luxury, the atmosphere is one of relaxed elegance. Casual yet neat attire is generally appropriate for common areas during the day. For dinner, particularly the kaiseki meal, smart casual wear is recommended. Guests are encouraged to wear the provided *yukata* robes within the resort premises, especially when transitioning to and from the onsen facilities, embracing a quintessential part of the Japanese ryokan experience.

**Tattoo Policy:** In Japan, tattoos have historically been associated with certain organizations, leading to restrictions at many public onsen. However, AMANE Resort Seikai's defining feature—the private, open-air onsen in every suite—largely mitigates this concern. Guests with tattoos can enjoy their personal bath without reservation. For use of any communal bathing facilities, while policies are evolving, it is always prudent to discreetly inquire with the resort directly upon booking or arrival about their current stance to ensure a comfortable experience for all. Many luxury properties are becoming more accommodating, but individual discretion remains key.

**Onsen Etiquette:** Even with private baths, a basic understanding of onsen etiquette is beneficial. Always rinse your body thoroughly before entering any bath, private or communal. Hair should be tied up and kept out of the water. Avoid bringing towels into the bathing water. The aim is to maintain the purity of the spring water for everyone's enjoyment.

**Language:** While the resort staff are accustomed to international guests, and English is often spoken at the front desk and in key service areas, having a basic phrasebook or translation app can enhance interactions, particularly when venturing into the local town.

By keeping these practical aspects in mind, guests can fully immerse themselves in the tranquil luxury and cultural richness that AMANE Resort Seikai so perfectly offers.

## Access

AMANE Resort Seikai, strategically located on the scenic coastline of Beppu, Oita Prefecture, offers relatively straightforward access for both domestic and international travelers.

### By Air

The most convenient airport for reaching Beppu is **Oita Airport (OIT)**. This regional airport offers connections to major Japanese cities like Tokyo (Haneda and Narita), Osaka (Itami), and Nagoya (Centrair), as well as some international routes.
- **From Oita Airport to Beppu:** The most efficient way to reach Beppu city center from Oita Airport is via the **Airport Limousine Bus**. These buses operate frequently and provide direct service to key locations in Beppu, including Beppu Kitahama (near Beppu Tower) or Beppu Station. The journey typically takes approximately 40-50 minutes.
- **From Beppu City Center to AMANE Resort Seikai:** From Beppu Station or Beppu Kitahama bus stop, the resort is a short taxi ride, usually taking about 5-10 minutes, depending on traffic. Some luxury resorts may offer shuttle services, so it is advisable to check with AMANE Seikai when booking your stay.

### By Train

Beppu is well-connected by Japan's extensive rail network, making it accessible from across Kyushu and beyond.
- **Nearest Station:** The primary train station is **JR Beppu Station**.
    - **From Fukuoka (Hakata Station):** Take a Limited Express Sonic or Nichirin train directly to Beppu Station. The journey is comfortable and typically takes around 2 hours.
    - **From Oita City:** Beppu is just a short 10-15 minute train ride from Oita Station on the JR Nippo Main Line.
- **From JR Beppu Station to AMANE Resort Seikai:** The resort is situated a convenient distance from the station. A taxi ride is the most practical option, taking approximately 5-10 minutes. Follow local signage or use a navigation app if walking, though a taxi is recommended for convenience, especially with luggage.

### By Car

For those exploring Kyushu by car, AMANE Resort Seikai is easily accessible from the Kyushu Expressway.
- **Directions:** Exit the Kyushu Expressway at the Beppu IC. From there, follow signs towards Beppu city center and the coastline. The resort is located directly on the waterfront. Parking facilities are available on-site for guests.

Regardless of your chosen mode of transport, the journey to AMANE Resort Seikai sets the stage for the tranquil and luxurious experience that awaits, culminating in the soothing embrace of Beppu's famed hot springs and the majestic ocean views.
