## Introduction

Beppu Showaen presents a distinguished proposition for those seeking the pinnacle of ryokan luxury coupled with the intimate indulgence of private onsen. Situated in Beppu, Oita Prefecture, a city globally celebrated for its abundant hot springs, Showaen distinguishes itself not merely by its location but by its unwavering commitment to an exclusive, tranquil experience. Far removed from the bustling public bathhouses, this esteemed establishment caters to discerning travellers who value privacy, impeccable service, and a deep appreciation for traditional Japanese aesthetics woven seamlessly with modern comforts. It is an address where the restorative power of Beppu's famed thermal waters is delivered with an unparalleled sense of personal seclusion and refined hospitality, rendering each visit a profoundly restorative sojourn into Japanese culture and relaxation.

## History & Tradition

The narrative of Beppu Showaen is one rooted deeply in the rich tradition of Japanese hospitality, *omotenashi*, and a respect for the natural bounty of Beppu. While specific founding dates can sometimes elude public records for such private establishments, the ryokan's pervasive atmosphere suggests a heritage stretching back decades, if not generations. Its very name, "Showaen," evokes a sense of the Showa era, a period often associated with a golden age of Japanese culture, resilience, and an understated elegance. This historical echo is palpable in the ryokan's architecture, its meticulously maintained gardens, and the enduring customs observed by its staff.

A commitment to preserving traditional craftsmanship is evident throughout the property, from the intricate woodworking in the guest rooms to the seasonal adjustments made to the *kaiseki* menu. The ryokan functions as a custodian of local Oita traditions, often sourcing ingredients from regional artisans and farmers, thereby contributing to the local economy and maintaining a tangible link to the land. Guests are not merely staying in a building, but immersing themselves in a living tradition, where the past is honoured, and the art of Japanese inn-keeping continues to thrive in its most refined form. This dedication ensures that Beppu Showaen offers more than just accommodation; it offers a curated cultural experience, steeped in heritage and executed with quiet distinction.

## Baths & Water

The essence of the Beppu Showaen experience is inextricably linked to its onsen, particularly the emphasis on private bathing. Beppu is renowned worldwide for its diverse hot springs, and Showaen draws directly from these mineral-rich subterranean sources. The water type is typically a simple thermal spring, often with subtle mineral qualities that are gentle on the skin yet profoundly invigorating. Guests often describe the water as exceptionally smooth, leaving the skin feeling supple and revitalised.

Each guest room at Beppu Showaen features its own private open-air or indoor onsen bath, a testament to the ryokan's dedication to exclusivity. These baths are typically fed by *kakenagashi*, a term signifying pure, free-flowing hot spring water that is continuously replenished and never recirculated. This ensures the utmost purity and potency of the thermal waters. The private outdoor baths are designed to harmonise with the surrounding natural beauty, often set within a secluded garden vista, allowing guests to soak while listening to the sounds of nature and feeling the gentle breeze. The materials used, such as fragrant cypress wood or natural stone, enhance the sensory experience, adding an element of serene luxury.

The privacy afforded by these individual baths is a significant draw, allowing guests to relax without concern for public etiquette or timing. The reputed health benefits of Beppu's springs, often cited as aiding circulation, relieving muscle tension, and improving skin conditions, are amplified in this intimate setting. The ritual of a private soak, perhaps at dawn as mist rises from the garden, or under a canopy of stars in the evening, becomes a deeply personal and meditative practice, embodying the restorative power of Japan's ancient hot spring culture at its most refined.

## Rooms & Architecture

The architecture and design of Beppu Showaen's guest rooms are a masterclass in traditional Japanese aesthetics fused with contemporary comfort. The ryokan typically comprises a limited number of meticulously designed rooms, ensuring an atmosphere of exclusivity and quietude. Each suite is often a standalone structure or a self-contained unit, connected by manicured pathways winding through a central garden, enhancing the sense of a private retreat.

The architectural style frequently leans towards *sukiya-zukuri*, characterised by its understated elegance, integration with nature, and use of natural materials. Rich, dark woods, soft tatami mat flooring, and delicate *shoji* paper screens create an immediate sense of warmth and authenticity. The layout of the rooms is thoughtfully designed, typically featuring a spacious main living area, a separate sleeping area with futon bedding (though some may offer Western-style beds), and a dedicated space for tea ceremony or relaxation.

However, the pièce de résistance in almost every room is the private onsen bath. Many rooms boast their own open-air bath (*rotenburo*) nestled within a small, private garden, offering direct views of the surrounding greenery, or perhaps a meticulously sculpted rock garden. Indoor private baths are also common, often crafted from aromatic cypress wood, filling the air with a subtle, soothing fragrance. These private bathing sanctuaries are seamlessly integrated into the room's design, providing an immediate and effortless connection to Beppu's thermal bounty. Modern amenities, while discreetly incorporated, ensure a comfortable stay, offering the best of both worlds: timeless tradition and contemporary convenience. The carefully curated art, calligraphy, and seasonal floral arrangements within each room further elevate the experience, transforming a mere stay into an aesthetic journey.

## Dining

Dining at Beppu Showaen is a pivotal component of the luxury ryokan experience, manifesting as an exquisite *kaiseki* meal that celebrates the regional bounty of Oita Prefecture and Kyushu. Typically served in a private dining room or within the comfort of the guest's suite, this multi-course traditional Japanese dinner is an artistic expression as much as it is a culinary one.

The chefs at Showaen are dedicated to showcasing the finest seasonal ingredients. Guests can anticipate a progression of dishes that highlight fresh seafood from the nearby Bungo Channel, including delicate *sashimi* and grilled fish, alongside premium local produce and, famously, Oita's renowned Bungo beef. This prized wagyu, known for its exceptional marbling and tenderness, often features as a centrepiece, prepared in a manner that accentuates its natural richness. Each course, from the refreshing *sakizuke* (appetizer) to the delicate *dessert*, is presented with meticulous attention to detail, reflecting the colours, textures, and flavours of the current season. Utensils, serving ware, and presentation are carefully chosen to enhance the visual appeal, turning each dish into a small edible landscape.

Breakfast, typically a traditional Japanese affair, is equally elaborate, featuring an array of small, nutritious dishes such as grilled fish, miso soup, steamed rice, and various pickled vegetables, designed to gently awaken the palate. The private setting for meals allows for an intimate and unrushed experience, allowing guests to fully savour each delicate flavour and appreciate the culinary artistry. Dietary restrictions are often accommodated with advance notice, reinforcing the bespoke nature of the ryokan's hospitality. The dining experience at Showaen is not merely sustenance; it is a profound immersion into Japan's culinary traditions, executed with grace and exceptional quality.

## Local Area

Beppu, the vibrant city where Beppu Showaen resides, is a destination globally synonymous with onsen. While the ryokan offers a complete sanctuary within its grounds, the surrounding area provides ample opportunity for exploration and engagement with this unique thermal landscape.

The most iconic attraction is the "Hells of Beppu" (*Jigoku Meguri*), a collection of eight spectacular hot springs that are too hot for bathing but showcase the dramatic geothermal activity of the region. Each "hell" offers a distinct visual spectacle, from the vibrant cobalt blue of Umi Jigoku (Sea Hell) to the bubbling mud pools of Onishi Bouzu Jigoku. These geological wonders are easily accessible and provide fascinating insight into Beppu's volcanic origins.

Beyond the "Hells," visitors can explore various public and semi-private bathing experiences, including the unique sand baths where one is buried in naturally heated volcanic sand. For nature enthusiasts, Takasakiyama Monkey Park, home to a large troop of wild Japanese macaque monkeys, offers an engaging encounter with wildlife. The elegant Beppu Tower provides panoramic views of the city, the bay, and the surrounding mountains.

The city also boasts a burgeoning arts scene, local crafts, and charming shopping streets where one can find regional delicacies and souvenirs. Local museums and traditional craft centres, such as those showcasing bamboo artistry (*Beppu Takezaiku*), offer glimpses into Oita's cultural heritage. Exploring Beppu provides a compelling contrast to the serene privacy of Showaen, offering a deeper understanding of the region's thermal heart and vibrant local life.

## Practical Tips

For a stay at Beppu Showaen, a few practical considerations can enhance the experience. Given its reputation and limited number of rooms, booking well in advance, particularly for peak seasons or weekends, is highly recommended. You will need to check when booking regarding specific availability and seasonal pricing.

Check-in times typically range from 3:00 PM to 6:00 PM, with check-out usually around 10:00 AM or 11:00 AM. It is advisable to confirm these timings directly with the ryokan. Upon arrival, guests are often greeted with a welcome tea and a brief introduction to the ryokan's facilities.

While staying, guests are encouraged to wear the provided *yukata* (a casual cotton kimono) throughout the property, fostering an authentic Japanese atmosphere and a sense of relaxation. Dining in *yukata* is perfectly acceptable. General ryokan etiquette, such as removing shoes before entering certain areas, should be observed.

Regarding tattoo policies, while many public onsen in Japan historically prohibit visible tattoos, luxury ryokans with private onsen, such as Beppu Showaen, are generally more accommodating. The provision of private in-room baths effectively bypasses most concerns, allowing guests with tattoos to enjoy the onsen experience without reservation. However, it is always prudent to discreetly inquire when making a reservation if you have extensive or highly visible tattoos and wish to use any common areas, though this is rarely an issue at properties of this calibre focusing on privacy. Guests typically settle their bills upon check-out, with major credit cards usually accepted, though it is wise to confirm payment options beforehand.

## Access

Beppu Showaen's location in Beppu makes it readily accessible, yet feels wonderfully secluded once you arrive.

**By Air:**
The most convenient air access is via Oita Airport (OIT). From Oita Airport, limousine buses operate regularly to Beppu City. The journey typically takes around 40-50 minutes. Fukuoka Airport (FUK) is another major international gateway; from there, direct highway buses connect to Beppu, a journey of approximately 2 to 2.5 hours.

**By Train:**
Beppu Station is the primary rail hub. The city is well-connected by JR Kyushu trains.
*   **From Fukuoka (Hakata Station):** Take the JR Limited Express Sonic or Nichirin trains directly to Beppu Station. The journey usually takes around 2 to 2.5 hours.
*   **From Oita City:** Beppu is just a short 10-15 minute train ride from Oita Station.

**From Beppu Station to Beppu Showaen:**
Once you arrive at Beppu Station, the ryokan is typically a short taxi ride away. A taxi journey will be the most convenient option, especially with luggage, and generally takes less than 10-15 minutes depending on traffic. You can also consult maps or the ryokan's official website for potential local bus routes, although a taxi offers the most direct and comfortable transfer to the tranquility of Showaen. Driving is also an option, with parking typically available on-site for guests.
