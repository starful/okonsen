## Introduction

To speak of Hakone is to conjure images of mist-shrouded mountains, the serene expanse of Lake Ashi, and the invigorating steam rising from its numerous hot springs. Yet, within this celebrated onsen town, a particular establishment stands as a beacon of enduring Japanese hospitality: Matsuzakaya Honten. For travelers seeking an onsen experience that transcends the superficial and embraces the very essence of traditional welcome, this esteemed ryokan offers an profound journey into the heart of Hakone's heritage.

Matsuzakaya Honten is not merely a place to rest; it is a living testament to centuries of refined innkeeping. What truly sets it apart, beyond its venerable history and exquisite offerings, is its remarkably progressive stance on hospitality: a warm and unambiguous embrace of all guests, including those with tattoos. In an industry where such openness is still a rarity, Matsuzakaya Honten champions an ethos of inclusion, allowing every visitor to immerse themselves fully and comfortably in its exceptional onsen and the quiet luxuries of a stay that feels deeply, authentically Japanese. Here, the weight of tradition is worn not as a burden of exclusion, but as an elegant garment of universal welcome.

## History & tradition

The foundations of Matsuzakaya Honten were laid in 1662, in the heart of the Edo period. For over 360 years, this ryokan has observed the ebb and flow of Japanese history from its tranquil perch in Hakone. Originally catering to feudal lords, samurai, and common travelers navigating the Tokaido road between Edo and Kyoto, Matsuzakaya Honten became a vital waypoint, a place of sanctuary and rejuvenation along arduous journeys. Its very existence is interwoven with the narrative of Japan's past, bearing witness to changing eras while steadfastly upholding its core mission of providing solace and exceptional service.

The ryokan's long lineage is palpable in its architecture, the arrangement of its gardens, and the meticulous attention to detail in its service. Generations of the same family have overseen its evolution, ensuring that the spirit of *omotenashi* – selfless, anticipatory hospitality – remains undiluted. While the world outside has transformed at a rapid pace, Matsuzakaya Honten has skillfully navigated modernity by preserving its heritage, infusing new comforts without sacrificing its soul. This deep respect for history extends to its philosophy of welcome, where true tradition is understood not as rigid adherence to outdated customs, but as an open heart ready to receive all who seek its comfort and healing. This inclusive ethos, notably extending to guests with tattoos, is a profound expression of its ancient roots, reflecting a time when travelers were simply travelers, and hospitality was paramount.

## Baths & water

The onsen experience at Matsuzakaya Honten is a journey into the restorative powers of Hakone's celebrated spring waters. The ryokan is blessed with access to two distinct types of natural hot springs, each offering unique therapeutic benefits and sensory delights.

The primary spring is a **sulfur spring**, characteristic of many of Hakone's volcanic sources. Guests will immediately notice its distinctive, slightly pungent aroma, a natural hallmark indicating the presence of beneficial minerals. The water, often milky-white or faintly opaque, feels remarkably soft and silky on the skin. Sulfur-rich waters are renowned for their purported ability to improve circulation, alleviate muscle aches, and promote skin health, leaving one feeling deeply cleansed and rejuvenated.

Complementing this is a **simple alkaline spring**, presenting clear, smooth water that is gentle on the skin. These waters are celebrated for their moisturizing properties and their soothing effect, often described as leaving the skin feeling supple and smooth, akin to a natural lotion. The combination of these two distinct water types provides a comprehensive and varied bathing experience, catering to different preferences and needs.

Matsuzakaya Honten offers a selection of bathing environments. The communal *daiyokujo* (large public baths) provide a spacious and traditional setting, allowing guests to fully appreciate the breadth and tranquility of the onsen experience. These often feature both indoor and outdoor *rotenburo* sections, allowing bathers to soak amidst the natural beauty of the surrounding gardens and the gentle sounds of the Hakone landscape.

Crucially, for those desiring privacy or for tattooed guests who prefer a more secluded experience, the ryokan offers several **private baths**. Some guest rooms are equipped with their own exquisite private rotenburo, allowing for an intimate soak at any time of day or night, framed by garden views. Additionally, several bookable private baths (kashikiri-buro) are available, providing an exclusive space for individuals, couples, or families to relax in complete solitude. This commitment to offering private bathing options, alongside its open tattoo policy in communal areas, ensures that every guest can enjoy the profound benefits of Matsuzakaya Honten's mineral-rich waters without reservation or discomfort. The therapeutic embrace of these ancient springs is truly open to all who seek their healing touch.

## Rooms & architecture

The guest rooms and overall architecture of Matsuzakaya Honten are a testament to classic Japanese aesthetics, seamlessly blending traditional design with thoughtful modern comforts. The ryokan primarily features authentic Japanese-style rooms, characterized by the serene simplicity and natural materials that define classic Japanese interiors.

Upon entering, guests are greeted by the warmth of polished wooden floors and the soft texture of tatami mats. *Shoji* paper screens and *fusuma* sliding doors create flexible spaces and offer a gentle diffusion of light, evoking a sense of calm and tranquility. Each room typically includes a *tokonoma* (alcove), often adorned with a seasonal floral arrangement and a calligraphic scroll, reflecting the subtle artistry of Japanese interior design. Traditional *zabuton* cushions and low tables invite guests to relax in comfort, while futons are laid out by staff in the evenings, transforming the living space into a cozy bedroom.

Many rooms boast picturesque views, whether overlooking the meticulously manicured traditional gardens, the tranquil flow of a stream, or the lush, verdant hills of Hakone. These natural vistas serve as living art, changing with the seasons and providing a constant connection to the outside world.

A notable feature for those seeking ultimate seclusion and luxury are the rooms equipped with private outdoor baths. These exclusive spaces allow guests to enjoy the onsen experience directly from their room, surrounded by nature and in complete privacy. The design of these private baths often integrates local stone and wood, creating a harmonious blend with the natural environment.

The architecture throughout the ryokan itself reflects its long history, with various wings and structures developed over centuries. While some sections feature more recent renovations to enhance comfort and accessibility, the core aesthetic remains deeply rooted in traditional Japanese craftsmanship. Intricate woodwork, elegant eaves, and a quiet, unassuming grandeur define its appearance. The communal areas, including the lobby and hallways, are often adorned with historical artifacts, traditional art, and carefully arranged displays that tell the story of the ryokan's past, creating an atmosphere that is both refined and deeply rooted in its heritage.

## Dining

Dining at Matsuzakaya Honten is an integral part of the ryokan experience, a celebration of regional flavors, seasonal ingredients, and meticulous culinary artistry presented through the tradition of *kaiseki*. This multi-course meal is not merely food; it is a performance for the senses, reflecting the changing seasons and the bounty of the Hakone region.

Each kaiseki dinner is a carefully orchestrated symphony of dishes, designed to delight with its variety of textures, temperatures, and flavors. Expect a procession of beautifully plated courses, beginning with a delicate *sakizuke* (appetizer) and progressing through an array of specialties. These typically include fresh sashimi, often sourced from the nearby Suruga Bay, showcasing pristine seafood; a warming soup; grilled fish or succulent regional meats; simmered dishes; a seasonal tempura selection; and a hearty rice dish, often accompanied by miso soup and Japanese pickles.

The chefs at Matsuzakaya Honten are masters of their craft, emphasizing local and seasonal ingredients. Vegetables are often sourced from nearby farms, and the pristine waters of Hakone contribute to the quality of ingredients used. The presentation is as vital as the taste, with each dish arranged like a miniature work of art, using vibrant colors, elegant dishware, and subtle garnishes to enhance the visual appeal.

Breakfast at the ryokan is equally a refined affair. Typically a traditional Japanese breakfast (*washoku*), it includes grilled fish, steamed rice, miso soup, various small side dishes, Japanese pickles, and often a delicate egg dish. This nourishing spread provides a gentle and satisfying start to the day, reflecting the same attention to detail and quality as the evening meal.

Meals are typically served in private dining rooms, allowing for an intimate and undisturbed experience, or in a beautifully appointed communal dining hall, depending on the room plan and guest preference. The dining environment, whether private or communal, is designed to enhance the culinary journey, allowing guests to fully savor the flavors and the ambiance. For those with dietary restrictions or allergies, it is always advisable to inform the ryokan when booking, as adjustments can often be made to accommodate specific needs, ensuring a delightful and safe dining experience for all.

## Local area

Matsuzakaya Honten is ideally situated in Hakone, a region renowned for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and diverse attractions. The ryokan's location provides a serene base from which to explore the best of what this iconic destination has to offer.

Just a short distance away, guests can immerse themselves in art and nature at the **Hakone Open-Air Museum**, an expansive outdoor gallery showcasing sculptures by renowned international and Japanese artists, set against a backdrop of rolling hills. For a more traditional cultural experience, the **Hakone Museum of Art** features exquisite Japanese ceramics and garden landscapes.

No visit to Hakone is complete without experiencing the scenic **Hakone Ropeway**, which offers breathtaking panoramic views of Mount Fuji (on clear days), Lake Ashi, and the volcanic vents of Owakudani, a dramatic landscape of sulfuric fumes and hot springs. A stop at Owakudani provides a unique opportunity to taste "black eggs" (*kuro-tamago*), eggs hard-boiled in the sulfurous hot springs, believed to extend one's life.

Lake Ashi itself is a hub of activity. Guests can embark on a charming cruise aboard one of the "pirate ships" or more traditional sightseeing boats, gliding past the iconic **Hakone Shrine** with its striking red *torii* gate seemingly floating on the water. The shrine itself, nestled amidst ancient cedar trees, offers a tranquil spiritual retreat.

For history enthusiasts, a visit to the **Hakone Checkpoint Museum** provides a fascinating insight into the Edo-period customs and strictures of travel along the Tokaido road. Nature lovers will appreciate the numerous hiking trails that wind through the Hakone National Park, offering opportunities for peaceful strolls or more challenging treks amidst lush forests and volcanic terrain.

Additionally, the area surrounding Matsuzakaya Honten itself encourages leisurely exploration, with charming local shops, cafes, and smaller onsen establishments contributing to the authentic Hakone atmosphere. The ryokan's accessible location allows guests to easily combine relaxation at the springs with cultural excursions, ensuring a comprehensive and enriching stay in this captivating region of Japan.

## Practical tips

To ensure a seamless and enjoyable stay at Matsuzakaya Honten, a few practical considerations are worth noting for prospective guests.

**Reservations:** As a historically significant and highly regarded ryokan, Matsuzakaya Honten can be quite popular, especially during peak seasons (spring cherry blossom, autumn foliage) and Japanese holidays. It is highly recommended to book well in advance, particularly if you have specific room preferences, such as a room with a private outdoor bath. Checking the ryokan's official website or reputable booking platforms for availability is always the best first step.

**Check-in/Check-out:** Typical check-in times for ryokans are usually in the afternoon, often around 3:00 PM, allowing staff ample time to prepare rooms to impeccable standards. Check-out is typically around 10:00 AM or 11:00 AM. It’s always best to confirm the exact times when making your reservation or upon arrival.

**Language:** While Japanese is the primary language, Matsuzakaya Honten, as a ryokan accustomed to international guests, typically has staff members who possess varying degrees of English proficiency. Basic communication for check-in, dining, and general inquiries is usually manageable. Politeness and patience, along with the use of translation apps if needed, can further enhance communication.

**Dress Code:** The atmosphere at a ryokan is generally relaxed. Guests are encouraged to change into the provided *yukata* (a casual cotton kimono) after check-in. It is perfectly acceptable, and indeed customary, to wear your yukata throughout the ryokan—to meals, to the baths, and while relaxing in your room. This contributes significantly to the immersive and traditional experience.

**Onsen Etiquette:** Japanese onsen etiquette is crucial for a respectful and comfortable experience for all guests. Always shower thoroughly and cleanse your body *before* entering the bathwater. Hair should be tied up and kept out of the water. Towels should not be brought into the main bathwater; small modesty towels can be used for discretion but should be placed on your head or at the side of the bath. The onsen is a place for quiet relaxation, so keep voices low.

**Tattoo Policy:** This is a paramount feature of Matsuzakaya Honten. Unlike many traditional ryokans in Japan, Matsuzakaya Honten proudly maintains an **open and welcoming policy towards guests with tattoos**. This means tattooed individuals are explicitly permitted to use both the communal *daiyokujo* (large public baths) and, of course, any private baths available. This progressive stance is a reflection of the ryokan's deep-rooted commitment to true *omotenashi* – universal hospitality. It signifies that all guests, regardless of appearance, are genuinely welcome to partake in the full onsen experience without concealment or discomfort. This policy is a significant differentiator and a key reason why many international travelers, particularly those with tattoos, seek out this historic establishment. It removes a common barrier to enjoying traditional Japanese culture and allows for a truly unburdened and authentic onsen escape.

## Access

Matsuzakaya Honten is located in the scenic Hakone area of Kanagawa Prefecture, making it a convenient escape from the bustling metropolises of Tokyo and beyond. Here’s how to reach this historic ryokan:

**From Tokyo (Shinjuku Station):**
*   **Odakyu Romancecar (Limited Express):** The most direct and comfortable option. Take the Odakyu Romancecar from Shinjuku Station directly to **Hakone-Yumoto Station**. The journey typically takes approximately 85-95 minutes.

**From Tokyo (Tokyo Station/Shinagawa Station):**
*   **Shinkansen to Odawara, then local train:** Take the Tokaido-Sanyo Shinkansen (Kodama or Hikari) from Tokyo Station or Shinagawa Station to **Odawara Station**. This rapid journey takes around 30-40 minutes.
*   From Odawara Station, transfer to the **Hakone Tozan Railway** for a scenic ride to **Hakone-Yumoto Station**. This local train takes about 15-20 minutes.

**From Hakone-Yumoto Station to Matsuzakaya Honten:**
Once you arrive at Hakone-Yumoto Station, there are a couple of primary ways to reach the ryokan:

1.  **Hakone Tozan Bus:**
    *   From Hakone-Yumoto Station, proceed to the bus terminal.
    *   Look for buses heading towards Kowakien, Moto-Hakone, or Togendai (typically routes H, K, T, or R).
    *   Alight at the **"Kowakien" bus stop**. The bus journey is approximately 20-30 minutes, depending on traffic and the specific route.
    *   From the Kowakien bus stop, Matsuzakaya Honten is typically a short walk (a few minutes). It is advisable to have a map or use a navigation app for the final approach.

2.  **Taxi:**
    *   Taxis are readily available outside Hakone-Yumoto Station. A taxi ride directly to Matsuzakaya Honten would be the most convenient, albeit more expensive, option, especially if you have luggage. The ride typically takes about 15-20 minutes.

**Driving:**
For those traveling by car, Matsuzakaya Honten provides parking facilities for its guests. The ryokan is accessible via local roads in the Hakone region, connecting to major highways. It's always recommended to use a GPS or navigation system for the most accurate driving directions.

It is always prudent to confirm public transport schedules and routes closer to your travel date, especially during holidays or adverse weather conditions. The staff at Hakone-Yumoto Station or the ryokan itself can also provide assistance with local directions.
