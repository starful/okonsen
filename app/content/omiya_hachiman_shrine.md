**Omiya Hachiman Shrine (Â§ßÂÆÆÂÖ´Âπ°ÂÆÆ)** in Higashi-Koiwa is a revered local sanctuary dedicated to **Emperor Ojin**, also known as the deity **Hachiman**. Since ancient times, Hachiman has been worshipped as a powerful guardian of the community, a god of archery and war, and more recently, a protector against misfortune and a patron of household safety.

The shrine serves as the *Ujigami* (guardian deity) for the Higashi-Koiwa district. While its exact founding date is shrouded in the mists of time, it has been a focal point of local faith since the Edo period. The "Omiya" in its name signifies its status as a "Great Shrine" within the local hierarchy, and the precinct is characterized by its dignified wooden architecture and the ancient trees that stand watch over the worshippers.

### 3. üìú Goshuin and Shrine Items: Traditional Blessings

At the shrine office, visitors can receive a hand-brushed **Goshuin** featuring the bold characters of "Omiya Hachiman." Being a Hachiman shrine, their amulets (*Omamori*) are particularly noted for "Shoyu-yoke" (protection against general misfortune) and academic success. Many local families visit during *Shichi-Go-San* to pray for the healthy growth of their children, a tradition that remains vibrant in this community.

***

### 4. üåÉ What to See Nearby: The Serene Edo River Bank

Just a short walk east from the shrine lies the majestic **Edo River (Edogawa)**. The riverbank offers expansive green spaces and cycling paths. It is an ideal spot to visit after your shrine prayer to enjoy a sunset walk or to see the local rowing clubs practicing on the water. During the spring, the cherry blossoms along the river are a sight to behold.
