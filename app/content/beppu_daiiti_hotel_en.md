## Introduction

While Beppu offers an unparalleled array of onsen experiences, Beppu Daiiti Hotel distinguishes itself through its dedication to providing exquisite private bathing facilities, a feature that naturally extends a warm invitation to all guests, including those who might otherwise navigate the complexities of public bath etiquette due to tattoos.

The essence of a Japanese onsen visit is relaxation, rejuvenation, and an immersion into a profound cultural practice. Beppu Daiiti Hotel endeavors to make this experience universally accessible, ensuring that the tranquility of a private soak can be enjoyed without reservation. From the moment one steps into its refined entrance, a sense of calm pervades, promising an escape into the soothing embrace of Beppu's famed mineral-rich waters. This property is not merely a place to stay; it is a gateway to experiencing the therapeutic power of Beppu's springs in an intimate, personal setting, championing a modern vision of hospitality that respects individual needs while honoring the timeless allure of the onsen.

Since its inception, the hotel has evolved, adapting to the changing needs of travelers while steadfastly upholding the core principles of a ryokan: impeccable service, a deep connection to local culture, and a profound respect for the onsen experience.

The hotel embodies a modern interpretation of Japanese inn-keeping, marrying traditional aesthetics with contemporary amenities. This is particularly evident in its architectural design and interior decor, which pay homage to classic Japanese craftsmanship while integrating elements that cater to today's discerning traveler. The commitment to private onsen, for instance, represents an innovative response to growing demand for personal space and comfort, a reflection of how the hotel thoughtfully adapts tradition to create a more inclusive and personalized experience for its guests. It acknowledges that the desire for an authentic onsen soak is universal, and that fostering an environment where guests feel entirely at ease is paramount to preserving the very spirit of relaxation that onsen are meant to provide. Through this approach, Beppu Daiiti Hotel continues to write its chapter in Beppu's rich onsen narrative, standing as a beacon of progressive tradition.

The heart of the Beppu Daiiti Hotel experience undoubtedly lies within its bathing facilities, particularly its celebrated private onsen. Beppu is blessed with an astounding variety of hot spring sources, and the hotel draws upon these natural endowments to offer an authentic and deeply therapeutic bathing experience. The water itself is a crucial element, sourced directly from Beppu’s geothermal springs, known for their unique mineral compositions and restorative properties. While specific water analyses can vary, Beppu's waters are typically rich in minerals like sodium chloride, bicarbonate, and sulfates, which are renowned for their benefits to skin health, circulation, and muscle relaxation. The sensation upon entering the bath is one of immediate warmth and a silky smoothness, indicative of the water’s quality.

Beppu Daiiti Hotel distinguishes itself by focusing predominantly on private bathing spaces, a thoughtful design choice that provides unparalleled comfort and privacy. The private baths themselves are meticulously crafted, often featuring natural materials such as *hinoki* cypress wood, renowned for its delicate aroma and smooth texture, or local stone, which retains heat beautifully and offers a grounding aesthetic. Some baths may also incorporate open-air elements, allowing guests to breathe in the fresh Beppu air while soaking, perhaps beneath a canopy of stars or the soft glow of morning light.

This emphasis on private onsen naturally extends an open invitation to guests who might typically face restrictions in communal baths due to visible tattoos. The hotel's provision of individual bathing spaces eliminates any potential discomfort or self-consciousness, allowing every guest to fully embrace the Japanese onsen tradition with complete peace of mind. Here, the focus is entirely on personal rejuvenation, the therapeutic embrace of the hot spring waters, and the quiet contemplation that such a private setting affords. ## Dining

A stay at Beppu Daiiti Hotel is incomplete without indulging in its culinary offerings, particularly the traditional Japanese multi-course dinner, known as *kaiseki*. This elaborate dining experience is a cornerstone of ryokan hospitality, renowned for its artistic presentation, seasonal ingredients, and meticulous preparation, transforming a meal into a sensory journey.

The kaiseki served at Beppu Daiiti Hotel typically showcases the rich bounty of Oita Prefecture, a region celebrated for its pristine seafood from the Seto Inland Sea and its high-quality agricultural products. Guests can anticipate a progression of small, exquisitely crafted dishes, each a testament to the chef's skill and creativity. The courses usually begin with an *sakizuke* (appetizer), followed by an *owan* (soup), *otsukuri* (sashimi), *yakimono* (grilled dish), *nimono* (simmered dish), and *agemono* (fried dish), among others. Each dish is carefully balanced in flavor, texture, and color, designed to delight both the palate and the eye.

A highlight of Oita's culinary scene, often featured in local kaiseki, is *Bungo-gyu*, the prefecture's acclaimed beef. Known for its tenderness and rich marbling, it might be presented as a perfectly seared steak, in a delicate shabu-shabu, or as part of a grilled dish. Fresh seafood, caught daily from nearby waters, also plays a starring role, with seasonal selections ensuring variety and peak freshness. Vegetables, often sourced from local farms, are prepared to highlight their natural flavors, offering a vibrant contrast to richer elements.

The dining experience at Beppu Daiiti Hotel typically takes place in either a dedicated dining area that may offer private or semi-private seating, or in the comfort of guests' own rooms, providing an intimate setting for savoring each course. The presentation is as vital as the taste, with dishes arranged artfully on bespoke ceramics and lacquerware, reflecting the changing seasons and adding to the overall aesthetic pleasure. Breakfast, usually a traditional Japanese set meal featuring grilled fish, rice, miso soup, and various small side dishes, offers a nourishing start to the day, ensuring guests are well-prepared for exploring Beppu. The hotel's commitment to quality dining ensures that every meal is a memorable part of the overall onsen ryokan experience.

## Local area

Beppu, the vibrant city where Beppu Daiiti Hotel is situated, is a destination unlike any other, synonymous with hot springs. Its geothermal activity is palpable, with steam rising from countless vents and bubbling pools visible throughout the urban landscape. Beyond the hotel’s private onsen, the city offers a diverse array of attractions that cater to every interest, inviting exploration and discovery.

One of Beppu’s most iconic attractions is the "Jigoku Meguri" or Hell Tour. These are not bathing onsen, but rather a series of spectacular, often vividly colored, geothermal hot springs that are too hot for bathing. Each of the nine "Hells" possesses a unique characteristic, from the crimson pond of Chinoike Jigoku (Blood Pond Hell) to the intense blue of Umi Jigoku (Sea Hell), often accompanied by plumes of steam. Visitors can observe these natural wonders, enjoy local treats cooked by the steam, and learn about the science and folklore behind them.

For those seeking more direct onsen experiences outside the private baths of the hotel, Beppu offers numerous public bathhouses, including the historic Takegawara Onsen, with its sand baths where guests are buried in naturally heated volcanic sand. While the Beppu Daiiti Hotel caters to privacy and inclusivity regarding tattoos, these public facilities typically adhere to traditional rules, so guests should be mindful if planning visits.

Nature lovers can ascend Mount Tsurumi via ropeway for panoramic views of Beppu Bay and the surrounding mountains, especially breathtaking during autumn foliage or cherry blossom season. Nearby, Takasakiyama Natural Zoological Garden is home to a thriving population of wild Japanese macaques, offering a unique opportunity to observe these fascinating creatures in their natural habitat.

The city itself is also a delightful place to wander, with numerous local eateries, souvenir shops offering onsen-related products, and craft stores showcasing Oita's artisanal heritage. From the steam-cooked delights of *jigoku mushi* cuisine to the serene beauty of local temples, Beppu promises a rich tapestry of experiences that perfectly complement a relaxing stay at Beppu Daiiti Hotel.

To ensure a seamless and enjoyable stay at Beppu Daiiti Hotel, particularly when leveraging its distinctive private onsen offerings, a few practical considerations are helpful.

While the hotel prioritizes private access, specific time slots for these exclusive baths are typically allocated, and securing your preferred time upon arrival or even when making your room reservation will enhance your experience. Regarding the hotel's inclusive approach to guests with tattoos, the primary facility enabling this is the provision of private onsen. Guests can bathe comfortably and without concern within their reserved private space. While the hotel cultivates an atmosphere of welcome, it is always respectful to use these private facilities as intended for personal enjoyment. There's no need for elaborate covering or concern about public perception within your private bath.

Upon check-in, guests will typically receive information about the hotel's amenities, dining schedules, and onsen availability. Familiarize yourself with these details to make the most of your stay. Japanese ryokan often have specific meal times for kaiseki dinner and breakfast; adhering to these schedules ensures the freshest and most authentic dining experience.

Footwear etiquette is also important. In most Japanese ryokan, including Beppu Daiiti Hotel, shoes are removed at the entrance, and guests are provided with slippers for moving around common areas. Within your room, you typically remove slippers before stepping onto tatami mats. Observing these customs is a simple way to show respect for the establishment and its cultural practices.

Finally, while the hotel staff are typically proficient in English, having a basic understanding of a few Japanese phrases can enhance communication. However, rest assured that the renowned Japanese hospitality ensures that language barriers rarely hinder a pleasant stay.

## Access

Reaching Beppu Daiiti Hotel, located in the heart of Beppu, Oita Prefecture, is relatively straightforward, whether traveling domestically within Japan or internationally.

**By Air:**
The most common point of entry for international visitors, or those traveling from distant parts of Japan, is Oita Airport (OIT). From Oita Airport, there are several convenient options to reach Beppu city center:
*   **Airport Bus:** Limousine buses operate frequently between Oita Airport and Beppu Kitahama bus stop, which is very close to Beppu Station. The journey typically takes around 40-50 minutes. This is often the most convenient and cost-effective option.
*   **Taxi:** Taxis are available outside the airport, offering a direct but more expensive route to the hotel.

**By Train:**
Beppu Station is a major railway hub and is easily accessible from various cities across Japan via the extensive JR network.
*   **From Fukuoka (Hakata Station):** The fastest and most comfortable way is to take the JR Limited Express Sonic train directly to Beppu Station. The journey typically lasts approximately 2 hours.
*   **From Hiroshima/Osaka/Tokyo:** Travelers can take the Shinkansen (bullet train) to Kokura Station (in Kitakyushu) or Hakata Station (in Fukuoka), and then transfer to the JR Limited Express Sonic for Beppu.

**From Beppu Station to Beppu Daiiti Hotel:**
Once you arrive at Beppu Station, the hotel is conveniently located:
*   **On Foot:** Depending on the exact location within Beppu, the hotel might be within walking distance (typically 5-15 minutes) from Beppu Station. Check the precise distance upon booking.
*   **Taxi:** Taxis are readily available outside Beppu Station, providing a quick ride directly to the hotel, especially useful if you have luggage. The ride would be very short, typically just a few minutes.
*   **Local Bus:** Beppu has an extensive local bus network. Several routes from Beppu Station typically pass near the hotel. Inquire at the station's tourist information center for the most current routes and stops.

The hotel's central location ensures that once you've arrived, exploring Beppu's many attractions, including its renowned "Hells" and various public onsen, is also easily managed on foot or via short local transport rides.
