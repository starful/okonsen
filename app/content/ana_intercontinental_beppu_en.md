## History & Tradition: Beppu's Fiery Past Meets Modern Splendor

Beppu, in Oita Prefecture, is a city synonymous with onsen, boasting one of the highest concentrations of geothermal hot spots in the world. Its history as a healing destination stretches back over a thousand years, with ancient texts documenting its powerful therapeutic waters. The city's unique landscape is punctuated by the dramatic "Hells of Beppu" (Jigoku Meguri), vibrant, steaming ponds of various mineral compositions that have captivated visitors for centuries, reflecting the earth's raw, untamed power. This deep-rooted tradition of onsen culture forms the very fabric of Beppu.

While the ANA InterContinental Beppu itself is a contemporary marvel, its essence is deeply interwoven with Beppu's rich heritage. The resort pays homage to this storied past by embracing the region's natural resources – its exceptional hot springs and breathtaking scenery – and integrating them seamlessly into a luxurious, world-class experience. It's a testament to how modern sophistication can coexist harmoniously with ancient traditions. The design philosophy draws inspiration from the natural surroundings and local craftsmanship, ensuring that while you enjoy cutting-edge amenities, you are always connected to the profound history and cultural significance of this extraordinary onsen town. The resort does not merely exist *in* Beppu; it enriches and celebrates Beppu’s enduring legacy as a sanctuary of wellness and natural wonder, offering a new chapter in its long history of welcoming travelers seeking rejuvenation.

## Things to Do Around the Area: Exploring Beppu's Charms

Beyond the unparalleled luxury and relaxation offered within the ANA InterContinental Beppu, the surrounding city of Beppu provides a wealth of unique attractions and experiences. Your stay is an ideal base for exploring the rich cultural and natural tapestry of this renowned onsen town.

A must-visit is the **Beppu Hells Tour (Jigoku Meguri)**, where you can marvel at seven spectacular, steaming hot springs, each with its own distinct color and character. Highlights include Umi Jigoku (Sea Hell) with its cobalt-blue waters, Chinoike Jigoku (Blood Pond Hell), known for its vivid red hue, and Tatsumaki Jigoku (Spout Hell), a geyser that erupts every 30-40 minutes. These natural wonders are not for bathing but offer a fascinating glimpse into the earth's powerful geothermal activity.

For breathtaking panoramic views that rival those from the resort, take the **Mount Tsurumi Ropeway** up to the peak of Mount Tsurumi. On clear days, you can see not only Beppu Bay but also Shikoku and the Chugoku region. The mountain is particularly stunning during autumn foliage or cherry blossom seasons.

Delve into Beppu's traditional onsen culture by visiting **Takegawara Onsen**, a historic public bathhouse dating back to 1879, famous for its elegant architecture and unique sand baths (sunayu) where you are buried in naturally heated sand. For art enthusiasts, the **Beppu Art Museum** showcases a collection of Japanese and Western art, while the **Beppu Global Tower** offers another vantage point for city views. Don't forget to explore the local streets, discover traditional crafts like Bamboo art, and sample local delicacies from charming food stalls, truly immersing yourself in the vibrant local atmosphere of this extraordinary hot spring city.

## FAQ & Practical Tips: Enhancing Your Luxury Onsen Experience

To ensure your stay at ANA InterContinental Beppu is as seamless and enjoyable as possible, here are some practical tips and answers to common questions:

**Tattoo Policy:**
Good news for guests with tattoos! Unlike many traditional Japanese ryokans, ANA InterContinental Beppu is a **tattoo-friendly** establishment. Guests with tattoos are welcome to use all public and private bathing facilities without concern. This inclusive policy allows everyone to fully enjoy the resort's magnificent onsen offerings.

**Best Season to Visit:**
Beppu is a captivating destination year-round.
*   **Spring (March-May):** Witness cherry blossoms (sakura) paint the landscape pink, offering stunning contrasts with the green hills and blue bay. Mild temperatures make it ideal for exploring.
*   **Summer (June-August):** Enjoy the vibrant energy of Beppu, with lively festivals and clear views of the bay. The resort's infinity pool is particularly inviting.
*   **Autumn (September-November):** Experience breathtaking autumn foliage (koyo) as the surrounding mountains transform into a riot of reds, oranges, and golds. The air is crisp, and visibility for views is often excellent.
*   **Winter (December-February):** Enjoy crisp, clear days with unparalleled visibility over the bay. Soaking in the warm onsen while surrounded by cool air is a truly invigorating experience.

**Booking Tips:**
*   **Book in Advance:** Especially during peak seasons (cherry blossom, Golden Week, Obon, New Year), rooms and private baths can be fully booked months ahead.
*   **Check Packages:** The resort frequently offers special packages that include kaiseki dinners, breakfast, and sometimes even exclusive access to certain facilities or local attractions.
*   **Direct vs. Third-Party:** While third-party sites offer convenience, booking directly with the hotel's official website often provides access to the best rates, exclusive offers, and the most flexible cancellation policies.
*   **Consider Weekdays:** If your schedule allows, visiting on weekdays can offer a more tranquil experience with fewer crowds.

**General Etiquette:**
Remember to rinse thoroughly before entering any onsen (public or private). While yukata (traditional Japanese robes) are often provided and encouraged for lounging within the resort, they are not typically worn outside the premises.
